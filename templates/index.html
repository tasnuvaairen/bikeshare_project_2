In 1965, Amsterdam implemented what is now considered the first bike share program in history. Although the project was a failure, it was an important first step. Only in the 2000s, and particularly within the last decade, that bike share would catch on.

China has the greatest number of bike share systems for a single country, but the majority are still located throughout Europe. And among all bike shares, Paris's Vélib is the largest in the world.

Citi Bike is New York City’s bike share system, and the largest in the nation. Citi Bike launched in May 2013 and has become an essential part of the city's transportation network.

People use bike share to commute to work or school, run errands, get to appointments or social engagements, and more. It's fun, efficient and affordable – not to mention healthy and good for the environment.
